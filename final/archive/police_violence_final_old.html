Violence by the Chicago Police Department

The Chicago Police Department (CPD) killing of Laquan McDonald in 2014 led to a city-wide reconning with violence at the hands of the CPD. The Invisible Institute filed a freedom of information act (FOI) and obtained CPD records of personelle, internal tactical reponse reports (TRR) which detail officer use of force, internal departmental complaints, and external civilian complaints from 2004 to 2016.

The following analysis explores how self-reported use of force (via TRR) and civilian complains are dispersed across the CPD roster.
